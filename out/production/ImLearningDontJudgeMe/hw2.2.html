Discription
Valor

A

1. Bonus Proficiencies: Members of the College of Valor gain proficiency with medium armor, shields, and martial weapons. This enhances their survivability and combat versatility. Players often appreciate the increased durability and combat options provided by these proficiencies. They feel more like frontline combatants and less like traditional spellcasters, which can be a refreshing change of pace.

2. Combat Inspiration: At 3rd level, bards gain the ability to use their Bardic Inspiration dice to bolster their allies' combat abilities. This allows them to add the inspiration dice to attack rolls and saving throws in addition to skill checks. Players and party members usually love Combat Inspiration. It's seen as a versatile tool that can turn the tide of battle and create memorable moments. Bards often become valuable support characters with this feature.

3. Extra Attack: Starting at 6th level, bards from the College of Valor gain the Extra Attack feature, allowing them to make two weapon attacks when taking the Attack action on their turn. This feature is highly valued by players who enjoy being in the thick of combat. It enhances their damage output and helps them fulfill the role of a frontline fighter effectively.

4. Countercharm: At 6th level, bards gain the Countercharm ability, which allows them to use their performance to grant advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed to themselves and their allies within 30 feet. Countercharm is seen as a useful utility feature. While not a flashy ability, it can save the party from potentially crippling conditions and provides a sense of protection.

5. Battle Magic: At 14th level, bards can cast a bard spell and make one weapon attack as a bonus action on the same turn. Battle Magic adds a significant damage boost to the bard's combat capabilities, making them even more formidable in battle. Players often appreciate the increased flexibility and damage output this feature provides.

Eloquence

S

1. Silver Tongue: At 3rd level, members of the College of Eloquence gain the Silver Tongue feature, which allows them to add their Bardic Inspiration dice to any Charisma (Persuasion) or Charisma (Deception) check without expending a Bardic Inspiration use. Players and Dungeon Masters often appreciate Silver Tongue for its role-playing potential. It enables the bard to be incredibly persuasive and manipulative in social interactions, making them valuable diplomats and negotiators.

2. Unsettling Words: Starting at 3rd level, bards can use Unsettling Words to reduce a creature's saving throw against a bard spell they cast by an amount equal to their Bardic Inspiration die. This feature is seen as a clever way to enhance a bard's spellcasting utility. Players like the idea of undermining an enemy's defenses before unleashing a potent spell, making it a strategic choice.

3. Universal Speech: At 6th level, bards gain the Universal Speech ability, allowing them to understand and be understood by any creature that speaks a language. They no longer need to rely on a shared language for communication. Universal Speech is often praised for its versatility, especially in diverse and foreign environments. It makes the bard an invaluable communicator and diplomat, bridging language barriers effortlessly.

4. Countercharm: At 6th level, bards gain the Countercharm feature, which allows them to use their performance to grant advantage on saving throws against being frightened or charmed to themselves and their allies within 30 feet. Similar to the College of Valor, Countercharm is appreciated for its utility in protecting the party from debilitating conditions. It enhances the bard's role as a support character.

5. Infectious Inspiration: At 14th level, bards gain Infectious Inspiration, which lets them use their Bardic Inspiration on themselves whenever they use it on an ally. Infectious Inspiration is often seen as a powerful capstone ability. It reinforces the bard's ability to inspire and motivate, even in the heat of battle, making them highly effective in both support and personal combat.
Whispers

B

1. Bonus Proficiencies: At 3rd level, members of the College of Whispers gain proficiency with a disguise kit, poisoner's kit, and one additional language of their choice. This reflects their secretive and covert nature. Players often appreciate these additional proficiencies, as they align with the subclass's theme of espionage and subterfuge. They offer unique role-playing opportunities and can be useful in various situations.

2. Bonus Proficiencies: Starting at 3rd level, bards can use their Bardic Inspiration to deal psychic damage to a creature they successfully frighten or charm using their class features or spells. Words of Terror adds a unique and menacing element to the bard's repertoire. Players find it interesting to use fear and charm effects offensively, combining crowd control with damage-dealing.

3. Mantle of Whispers: Universal Speech: At 6th level, bards gain the Universal Speech ability, allowing them to understand and be understood by any creature that speaks a language. They no longer need to rely on a shared language for communication. Universal Speech is often praised for its versatility, especially in diverse and foreign environments. It makes the bard an invaluable communicator and diplomat, bridging language barriers effortlessly.

4. Shadow Lore: At 6th level, bards gain the Shadow Lore feature, which lets them use their Bardic Inspiration to hinder a creature's abilities. It imposes disadvantage on their next ability check, attack roll, or saving throw. Shadow Lore is seen as a potent control ability, allowing bards to disrupt enemy actions at critical moments. Players appreciate its tactical utility, especially in challenging encounters.

5. Enthralling Performance: At 14th level, bards gain the Enthralling Performance feature, which allows them to charm an audience of their choice as though they cast the dominate person spell. Enthralling Performance is considered a powerful capstone feature. It can be a game-changer in social and diplomatic situations, and players often find it satisfying to use their bardic talents to manipulate important figures.
