Subclass

Tier

Discription
Light

B

1. Bonus Cantrip: At 1st level, Light Domain clerics gain the Light cantrip for free, which allows them to create light sources at will. This bonus cantrip is appreciated for its utility, as it provides a source of light in dark places, eliminating the need for torches or lanterns. It also sets the thematic tone for the subclass.

2. Warding Flare: Starting at 1st level, Light Domain clerics can use their reaction to impose disadvantage on an attack roll targeting them or an adjacent creature, thanks to their ability to interpose divine light. Warding Flare is highly regarded for its defensive capabilities. Players and party members often praise this feature for its ability to protect against incoming attacks, making Light Domain clerics effective frontline support.

3. Channel Divinity: Radiance of the Dawn: At 2nd level, Light Domain clerics gain the Channel Divinity feature, Radiance of the Dawn, which allows them to unleash a burst of radiant energy, damaging hostile creatures within a certain radius. Radiance of the Dawn is considered a powerful and thematic feature. Players enjoy the ability to deal radiant damage to multiple foes, especially useful against undead and creatures vulnerable to radiant damage.

4. Improved Flare: Starting at 6th level, Warding Flare's effectiveness improves. Clerics can now use it to impose disadvantage on saving throws as well as attack rolls, enhancing their ability to protect themselves and allies. Improved Flare is often seen as a significant upgrade to an already valuable defensive tool. Players appreciate the broader utility it provides in various combat situations.

5. Potent Spellcasting: At 8th level, Light Domain clerics gain the Potent Spellcasting feature, which allows them to add their Wisdom modifier to the damage they deal with cantrips. This feature enhances the cleric's damage-dealing potential and makes their cantrips more formidable. Players often find it useful in maintaining their relevance in combat encounters.
War

A

1. Bonus Proficiencies: At 1st level, War Domain clerics gain proficiency with martial weapons and heavy armor, emphasizing their role as frontline combatants. Players often appreciate the bonus proficiencies as they make the cleric more capable in combat. This feature sets the tone for the subclass's martial focus and is valued for its durability.

2. War Priest: Starting at 1st level, War Domain clerics can use a bonus action to make an additional weapon attack when they take the Attack action. War Priest is highly regarded for its ability to increase a cleric's damage output. Players enjoy the option to make multiple attacks in a single turn, especially when combined with powerful spells.

3. Channel Divinity: Guided Strike: At 2nd level, War Domain clerics gain the Channel Divinity feature, Guided Strike, which allows them to add +10 to an attack roll after seeing the initial roll but before knowing the outcome. Guided Strike is seen as a powerful and reliable ability to ensure critical hits or accurate strikes, making it a highly valuable feature in combat situations.

4. Channel Divinity: War God's Blessing:Also at 2nd level, War Domain clerics gain the War God's Blessing feature, which lets them use Channel Divinity to add +10 to an ally's attack roll. War God's Blessing is appreciated for its support potential. It allows the cleric to boost an ally's attack, providing versatility in both offense and defense.

5. Divine Strike:At 8th level, War Domain clerics gain the Divine Strike feature, which adds additional damage (usually based on the cleric's choice of damage type) to one weapon attack per turn. Divine Strike is valued for increasing the cleric's damage output, making them more effective in combat situations. Players often appreciate the flexibility to choose the type of damage.
Trickster

C

1. Bonus Spells: Trickery Domain clerics gain access to a unique set of spells, including spells like Charm Person, Mirror Image, and Pass without Trace, which emphasize their deceptive and stealthy nature. Players often appreciate the thematic spell selection, as it aligns with the trickster archetype. These spells provide a range of utility and misdirection, making Trickery Domain clerics excellent at subterfuge and support.

2. Blessing of the Trickster: At 1st level, Trickery Domain clerics can use their action to grant advantage on Stealth checks to a willing creature other than themselves. Blessing of the Trickster is seen as a helpful and versatile ability, especially for party members who rely on stealth. Players like the ability to support their allies in sneaky endeavors.

3. Channel Divinity: Invoke Duplicity: At 2nd level, Trickery Domain clerics gain the Channel Divinity feature, Invoke Duplicity, which allows them to create an illusory duplicate of themselves. This duplicate can be used for various deceptive tactics. Invoke Duplicity is often praised for its utility in combat and role-playing scenarios. Players enjoy the ability to confuse enemies and enhance their own stealth or attack options.

4. Channel Divinity: Cloak of Shadows: Also at 2nd level, Trickery Domain clerics gain the Cloak of Shadows feature, which lets them use Channel Divinity to become invisible until they make an attack or cast a spell. Cloak of Shadows is highly regarded for its versatility. It allows the cleric to vanish from sight, making them excellent at sneaking past enemies or positioning for surprise attacks.

5. Divine Strike: At 8th level, Trickery Domain clerics gain the Divine Strike feature, which adds poison damage to one weapon attack per turn. Divine Strike is valued for its damage enhancement, adding a thematic element of poison to the cleric's attacks. Players appreciate the flexibility it offers in dealing damage.
